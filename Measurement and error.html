Accuracy vs. Precision

Master how close your experimental measurements are to the true value, and to each other.

Simulation Engine Active

Accuracy (準確度)

How close a measured value is to the true value. High accuracy means there is very little systematic error (bias is small).

Precision (精確度)

How close multiple measured values are to each other under unchanged conditions. High precision means very little random error (the spread of results is small).

How to Calculate the Maximum Estimated Error

In physical science, when using a ruler, there is an alignment uncertainty of $\pm \frac{D}{2}$ at the zero start-point, and a reading uncertainty of $\pm \frac{D}{2}$ at the end-point. Combining both gives a total Maximum Estimated Error of the measurement equal to the smallest scale division ($D$):

Smallest Division ($D$) $1.0 \text{ cm}$
+
Uncertainty Formula $\text{Max Error} = \pm D$
=
Calculated Error Margin $\pm 1.0 \text{ cm}$
